## 1. Organizational Overview

Purgatoria is a **multi-layered community and open collaboration ecosystem** focused on communication resilience, technical literacy, and public readiness. 
At its core, it exists to build and maintain **communication infrastructure and ad-hoc systems in a state of readiness for high-stakes environments**, especially for **disaster relief, emergency response, and degraded-network operations**.

### 1.1 Purgatoria as a Community
Purgatoria is an independent community build as "communitas" a highly cohesive, non-hierarchical social structure bound by a shared commitment to knowledge distribution, advanced technical literacy, and a culture of collective production. 
We treat communication infrastructure not merely as a technical asset, but as a critical civic right.

As a community, we operate with the structure and discipline of a formal institution, transcending the conventions of traditional hobbyist groups. 
By adhering to the internal protocols and policies established within our organizational culture, we function with a high degree of professional rigor akin to that of a public agency or a corporate entity. 
This systematic approach enables us to achieve significantly faster execution and more sustainable, high-impact outcomes compared to previous initiatives.

### 1.2 Purgatoria as a Project
Purgatoria is an independent **research and development initiative**. We develop a wide range of projects aimed at establishing decentralized, grid-independent communication networks, while simultaneously identifying and contributing to existing initiatives to enhance the functionality and reliability of conventional amateur radio systems.

Other hand we investigate, deploy, and field‑test open‑source technologies across amateur radio, mesh networking, and disaster communications.
Based on the experience we’ve gained throughout this process, we modify the projects we’ve utilized to meet our specific needs and develop integrations to connect them all in the most appropriate ways.

Our current stack for establishing decentralized and grid-independent networks relies on open-source solutions including [Meshtastic](https://github.com/meshtastic), [Reticulum Network](https://github.com/markqvist/reticulum), [AREDN](https://github.com/aredn), [Wi-Fi HaLow](https://), and [Open MaNet](https://github.com/openmanet).

In addition to these structural implementations, Purgatoria operates as an open, collaborative incubation matrix, providing both the infrastructure and the social frameworks necessary for operators to engineer technical projects either autonomously or via joint working syndicates.

This architecture democratizes research and development visibility, enabling newly licensed amateur radio operators, junior developers, and independent researchers who lack established professional networks to amplify their operational footprint, interface with expansive technical circles, and secure optimal peer validation for their contributions.

Most fundamentally, this ecosystem institutionalizes systematic knowledge cross-pollination and decentralized peer-to-peer mentorship. 
Operators encountering critical engineering bottlenecks or development friction can seamlessly leverage the cross-disciplinary acumen of the wider collective, allowing them to overcome complex technical dependencies, optimize code or hardware performance, and efficiently drive stalled initiatives to final field deployment.

### 1.3 Purgatoria as an Infrastructure Layer
Purgatoria’s primary objective is to develop ad-hoc systems ready for immediate deployment and to train their operators, while simultaneously establishing permanent infrastructure in parallel. 
We train our field teams to assemble, configure, and operate transportable communication hubs within minutes of a catastrophic grid collapse or sudden humanitarian crisis, ensuring immediate operational readiness when conventional utilities fail.

Purgatoria’s long-term infrastructure goal is to establish **decentralized, green-energy-powered, grid-independent, disaster-resilient communication systems** with an immediate focus on **Turkey**, while building a model that can be replicated elsewhere.

In Purgatoria, we don’t blindly deploy the nodes that will form the communication infrastructure, nor do we deploy them haphazardly just because we see a high AGL or clear LOS.
Every permanent infrastructure deployment follows a rigorous three-phase pipeline:

1.  **RF Simulation:** The target deployment zone undergoes thorough software-based RF propagation simulation with high-resolution and up-to-date datasets. 
Coverage maps, link budget analyses, interference assessments, and terrain diffraction modeling are completed before any physical work begins.
3.  **Field Survey:** Physical site surveys are conducted by field operators — terrain exploration, ground truth validation of simulation outputs, physical access assessment, solar exposure measurement, perform spectrum monitoring for noise measurement and generate a heatmap based on the results. This process determines the environmental hardening requirements.
4.  **Node Preparation & Deployment:** A custom, **climate-hardened, stress-tested node** is built specifically for the target environment and permanently deployed. No off-the-shelf configuration is assumed to be sufficient; every deployment is treated as a specialized engineering problem.


### 1.4 Purgatoria as an Educational Platform
Purgatoria operates as a multidisciplinary educational platform designed to eradicate exclusionary technical gatekeeping. 
Rooted deeply in amateur radio ethics and foundational electromagnetic theory, our mission is to build highly qualified operators who possess the pedagogical skills to mentor the next generation.

In addition, by producing content in Turkish, English, and Chinese, we bring people from other countries together through amateur radio and build a cultural bridge between the communities that speak these languages.

To maximize independent, self-driven learning, Purgatoria structures its knowledge production across three complementary layers:

-   **PGC (Professional Generated Content):** structured, peer-reviewed technical reference manuals and formal knowledge artifacts.
-   **EGC (Employee/Core Team Generated Content):** in-depth research updates, internal operations documentation, and core project records.
-   **UGC (User Generated Content):** peer-to-peer troubleshooting, forum discussions, member experiences, and personal media amplification.

These layers are not redundant. They serve distinct learning modes and trust levels, ensuring that knowledge is both rigorous and accessible. 
At every level, our principle remains the same:
Equal access to knowledge is a fundamental right, and every individual deserves an equitable voice.


### 1.5 Purgatoria as a Knowledge-Creating Entity
Purgatoria is fundamentally an epistemic, **knowledge-generating organism**. We do not operate as standard technical copywriters, translators, or passive information aggregators. 
Our contribution to the literature is the active manufacturing of new technical paradigms.
And our source here is the cycle of engineering, deployment, failure, adaptation, and documentation.

-   We develop **novel communication systems**, including protocol wrappers, data serialization formats, and message routing frameworks not found in upstream projects, tailored specifically to the demands of civil resilience and disaster response.
-   We discover **novel use cases** for legacy radio technologies that decades of conventional amateur radio practice left unexplored, making technologies that predate the internet functional, relevant, and highly efficient in contemporary crises.
-   We produce **novel structural frameworks** for deploying and managing decentralized infrastructure at the community scale such as governance models, deployment procedures, inter-agency coordination protocols that represent genuine intellectual contributions, not adaptations of existing work.

The information generation in mention takes place separately and continuously at every stage.
From reviewing projects and conducting proof-of-concept tests to testing under various conditions, modifying them as needed, and creating technical documentation.

### 1.6 Purgatoria as a Service Provider
Purgatoria also functions as a service layer for its members, provisioning infrastructure over RF or secure network layers. These services may include:

-   decentralized **VoIP / telephony**,
-   secure **off-grid email matrices**,
-   localized **messaging systems**,
-   secure **storage hubs**,
-   and coordination platforms such as **TAK (Team Awareness Kit)** and similar GIS or ticketing tools for Search and Rescue (SAR) operations.

These services are built by the community, for the community. No single individual, corporate entity, or administrative body owns or controls the infrastructure.
The operational model is one of **crowdsourced mutual aid**, the Turkish concept of _"İmece"_, collective labor toward a shared public good, applied to communication infrastructure.

Architecturally, our services are completely distributed, containerized, and decoupled. Every service node is engineered with autonomous failover capabilities; if a major sector or regional hub suffers catastrophic failure, adjacent segments automatically scale and route traffic to absorb the disruption, guaranteeing high-availability uptime.

### 1.7 Purgatoria as an Ecosystem
At the highest level of abstraction, Purgatoria is a **neutral, open-source collaboration ecosystem** — the connective framework that makes cooperation possible between entities that would otherwise remain isolated or actively fragmented.

We serve as an **architectural bridge**—a **“Demilitarized Zone (DMZ)”**—uniting fragmented amateur radio clubs, isolated amateurs, and other civil associations. 
By providing neutral tooling, frameworks, and arbitration, we enable cooperation without bureaucratic friction.

This neutrality is safeguarded by **Open Governance** and **Radical Transparency**, ensuring that collaboration remains equitable, accountable and prevents that no single faction can monopolize the collective output.

### 1.8 Purgatoria as Standards Organization
Purgatoria functions as a community-driven standards body for amateur radio and telecommunications practice within its own operational ecosystem. 
While it is not a statutory authority on the level of institutions such as BTK, FCC, CEPT, ITU, AMSAT, or ARDC, it nevertheless develops, evaluates, and publishes internal standards intended to improve communication reliability, technical consistency, and operational efficiency across the Purgatoria community. 

These standards are not arbitrary conventions; they are practical frameworks created to ensure that the systems, procedures, and deployments used by members remain coherent, interoperable, and fit for purpose in real-world communications environments.

This standardization mechanism is driven by a highly diversified, cross-disciplinary matrix of professionals encompassing RF engineers, software developers, emergency communications experts, and formal delegates from allied amateur radio associations.
These panels convene periodically to evaluate existing technical bottlenecks and research optimization strategies.

Relevant topics are periodically reviewed by knowledgeable members, discussed in depth, and developed through research and iterative refinement. 
Draft standards are then tested, observed, and evaluated in practice, with notes recorded and improvements applied where necessary. 
Once a draft has matured, it is presented to the community for review and voting. 
After further refinement, the resulting standard is formally adopted, documented, and shared through the appropriate public channels for use by the wider community.

### 1.9 Purgatoria as an Interface Organisation
The administrative layer of Purgatoria functions as a specialized intermediary interface, bridging the operational gap between state regulatory institutions, non-governmental civil defense organizations, private sector enterprises, and the general public.

The practical consequence of this intermediary function is accelerated resolution: issues that might persist unaddressed for months when pursued by an individual acting without institutional standing are processed materially faster when channeled through an organized body with established external relationships and a documented governance record.

By positioning itself as a reliable public-private-civic conduit, Purgatoria optimizes inter-agency coordination while simultaneously serving as a public relations bridge for its external partners. 
It helps public institutions and private enterprises safely align their technical deployments with civilian expectations, protecting corporate and institutional reputations while fostering radical transparency and civic engagement.

Furthermore, this interface model extends directly into supply-chain optimization and aggregated market procurement. 
Due to its expansive operational footprint, the Purgatoria ecosystem frequently acts as the single largest bulk consumer and procurement anchor for specialized telecommunications components, hardware modules, and off-grid power infrastructure.

The management team leverages this collective bargaining power to source, audit, and negotiate optimal procurement pathways, mitigating supply chain vulnerabilities for individual operators. 
This entire financial and material acquisition pipeline is conducted transparently alongside the community within the dedicated `purgatoria/shopping` repository, converting collective economic scale into accessible, field-tested assets for the general public.

### 1.10 Purgatoria as a Consultancy
Purgatoria also provides **pro-bono** expert technical consultancy, regulatory compliance alignment, secure and resilient network architecture designs to organizations whose missions align with our values.
Including non govermental organizations, search-and-rescue organizations, local municipalities, law enforcement bodies, and corporate entities operating in critical infrastructure or humanitarian response contexts.

However, our institutional relationships always remain **secondary to the civilian public**. Purgatoria’s first responsibility is education, empowerment, and capability-building for individuals and communities. Institutional engagement is valuable, but it is never allowed to override the core mission of human-centered technical development.
Ultimately, it should not be forgotten that the motivation behind the establishment of Purgatoria was the incompetence and ineffectiveness of these institutions.

The optimal and expected path for any institution seeking our expertise is **organic embedding**: partner organizations must embed their own personnel directly into the Purgatoria community. 
These individuals are then trained from first principles by our community mentors, turning them into highly capable, internal communication assets for their respective institutions while strengthening the overarching public knowledge network.

## 2. Institutional Structure

The Purgatoria Organisation possesses a multi-dimensional, hybrid institutional architecture engineered to seamlessly integrate its grassroots decentralized community layer with formal legal, fiscal, commercial, and academic frameworks. 

This comprehensive ecosystem structurally decouples yet operationally coordinates seven distinct vectors under a unified strategic vision: a non-hierarchical human infrastructure (Community), a radically transparent financial tracking engine (Collective), a legally recognized regulatory and civil defense vehicle (Association), an international grant and cross-border compliance gateway (Foundation), an industrial risk-absorbing commercial R&D arm (Incorporated), an immutable scientific data archiving wing (Research Collective). 

This modular compartmentalization preserves maximal operational agility across the ecosystem while providing advanced liability decoupling, regulatory resilience, and institutional anti-fragility against systemic vulnerabilities.

### 2.1 Purgatoria Community
Purgatoria is a diverse collective of amateur radio enthusiasts, ranging in age from 12 to over 80, encompassing a vast array of expertise including engineers, academics, software developers, public officials, law enforcement officers, corporate leaders, legal and medical professionals, emergency responders, educators, and students.

Despite this professional diversity, Purgatoria operates on a foundation of absolute equality: regardless of age, occupation, or professional rank, every member stands on the same level, united by our shared identity as amateur radio operators. 

Purgatoria is a community forged by the collective consciousness of its members. Within our ranks, there is no hierarchy; group administrators hold no seniority over members, and there is no distinction in status between those who have been with us for years and those who joined yesterday.

We are a coalition of individuals united by a singular purpose: to bridge our respective gaps in knowledge, to educate future generations, and to achieve meaningful impact for our country and the world. 
By compensating for one another’s weaknesses and acting in synergistic cooperation, we operate with a speed and rate of success that is unparalleled.

### 2.2 Purgatoria Collective
Purgatoria Collective is the community-facing coordination layer of the ecosystem, structured through the [**Open Collective**](https://opencollective.com/purgatoria) framework to support radical transparency, distributed participation, and public accountability.

Its function is to aggregate grassroots support into a coherent financial and operational engine, enabling crowd-sourced funding, shared resource pooling, and open ledger maintenance in a manner that preserves institutional memory and makes every transaction auditable in real time by the wider community.

This architecture ensures that no financial decision can be siloed, obfuscated, or retroactively altered without immediate detection, anchoring the organization’s economic integrity in mathematically auditable public record rather than in institutional trust alone.

It operates without independent legal personality, functioning instead under the fiscal hosting umbrella provided by Open Collective's established legal and compliance infrastructure, thereby enabling the community to receive and disburse funds at organizational scale without the administrative overhead of independent entity registration. 

This arrangement preserves maximum operational agility at the community layer while maintaining full compliance with applicable financial reporting obligations through the host entity's established regulatory posture.

### 2.3 Purgatoria Association (TBD)
Purgatoria Association is the formally registered, non-profit Amateur Radio Association that constitutes the legally recognized operational shell for spectrum-related activity, repeater deployment, and regulated radio infrastructure. 

Under state regulatory requirements, this entity is necessary to lawfully secure RF allocations, construct and operate physical radio repeaters, and maintain the compliance posture required for licensed amateur radio operations and emergency communications work.

Furthermore, the Association operates as the recognized legal signatory empowered to execute formal, binding MoUs and operational protocols with national civil defense agencies and telecommunications authorities. 
This legal framework ensures that Purgatoria’s deployment grid can be seamlessly integrated into public safety networks during emergency disaster communications.

### 2.4 Purgatoria Foundation (TBD)
Purgatoria Foundation is the international fiscal hosting and compliance layer designed to absorb cross-border support, manage foreign-currency inflows, and maintain regulatory clarity across multiple jurisdictions. 

Its purpose is to provide a legally coherent gateway for grants, micro-patronage, and digital donations originating outside the domestic operating environment, while insulating local entities from unnecessary tax complexity, currency friction, and jurisdictional exposure.

This structure is especially important for international grants, institutional philanthropy, and borderless contribution models that require disciplined financial stewardship. 

By centralizing foreign-facing compliance and fiscal hosting within a dedicated layer, Purgatoria can preserve operational continuity, remain compliant across jurisdictions, and protect domestic nonprofit and community structures from avoidable legal and accounting burden.

### 2.5 Purgatoria Incorporated (TBD)
Purgatoria Incorporated is the commercial research, development, and manufacturing arm of the ecosystem, structured as a liability-buffering corporate entity such as an LLC or Inc. 

Its role is to contain industrial risk, manage hardware procurement and production pipelines, and provide the legal perimeter required for commercial operations that should not be absorbed by the nonprofit association or by individual operators. 

This separation is critical for liability decoupling, contractual clarity, and operational resilience.

It provides the legal infrastructure required to sign formal Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDAs) and interface seamlessly with commercial enterprises and state contractors, allowing the ecosystem to engage in high-level defense and infrastructure projects.

### 2.6 Purgatoria Research Collective
Purgatoria Research Collective constitutes the academic and scientific archival wing of the ecosystem, structured around the [**Zenodo**](https://zenodo.org/communities/purgatoria) open-access repository network operated under the auspices of CERN and the European OpenAIRE infrastructure.

Its primary function is the formal archiving of open-access research papers, RF engineering documentation, network telemetry datasets, folklore preservation materials, and related technical studies under permanent Digital Object Identifiers (DOIs). 

By publishing under persistent identifiers and maintaining versioned, open metadata, the Research Collective prevents scientific erasure and enables independent verification, reproducibility, and academic citation. 

The Collective’s archival practice enforces data governance standards, metadata completeness, and retention policies that satisfy academic, funder, and regulatory expectations for research integrity and long‑term stewardship.

### 2.7 TA Mesh Sub-Community
[TA Mesh](https://www.facebook.com/tamesh.org) or "[Meshtastic Turkiye Toplulugu](https://www.facebook.com/groups/tamesh)" serves as our specialized sub-community, bridging the gap between experienced amateur radio operators and newcomers, while focusing entirely on the proliferation, deployment, and optimization of decentralized off-grid mesh networks across the region.

The areas that Purgatoria focuses on in particular with TA Mesh are as follows:

-   **Localizes and deploys Meshtastic infrastructure** across Turkish geography, adapting hardware configurations, channel plans, and deployment strategies to local terrain, regulatory requirements, and community needs.
-   **Supports community members with hardware** through device advisory programs, group procurement initiatives, and hands-on configuration assistance — lowering the barrier to entry for operators new to mesh networking.
-   **Develops localized hardware solutions**, including antenna designs optimized for Turkish geographic conditions, custom enclosures for field deployment, and power systems adapted for the region's climate and grid reliability patterns.
-   **Pioneers decentralized, off-grid mesh network coverage** in areas underserved by conventional infrastructure, with a particular focus on building regional backbone nodes that can sustain mesh connectivity during extended grid failures.
-   **Serves as a living testbed** for the broader Purgatoria community, providing real-world data, field reports, and operational lessons that inform our documentation, training, and advocacy work.

TA Mesh helps translate Purgatoria’s broader philosophy into regional action. It is where open, low-power, infrastructure-light communication becomes tangible for people who need local resilience, practical training, and a shared technical language.

In the long term, TA Mesh is intended to help establish a healthier regional ecosystem around decentralized communications: not only by using the technology, but by helping shape the culture, support the hardware pipeline, and encourage community ownership of the stack.
